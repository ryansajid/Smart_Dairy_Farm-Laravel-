## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### 1.1 Purpose and Scope of This Document

This Business Requirements Document (BRD) represents the comprehensive blueprint for Smart Dairy Ltd.'s digital transformation journey through the implementation of the Smart Web Portal System and Integrated Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) platform. This document synthesizes requirements from multiple sources including the Request for Proposal (RFP), extensive business portfolio analysis spanning four strategic verticals, comprehensive market research, stakeholder interviews, and industry best practices research.

The BRD serves as the authoritative reference for all project stakeholders, establishing the foundation for a transformative digital ecosystem that will enable Smart Dairy to evolve from a single-farm operation producing 900 liters of milk daily from 255 cattle into a BDT 100+ Crore integrated dairy enterprise serving 10,000+ farming families and processing 15,000+ liters of milk daily by Year 3.

### 1.2 Transformation Vision

Smart Dairy stands at a pivotal inflection point in Bangladesh's dairy sector evolution. With the country facing a chronic 56.5% milk deficit (approximately 7.94 million metric tons annually), there exists an unprecedented opportunity for a technology-enabled, quality-focused dairy enterprise to capture significant market share while creating positive social impact for rural farming communities.

The Smart Web Portal System and Integrated ERP will serve as the digital backbone enabling this transformation, connecting four distinct but interrelated business verticals:

1. **Dairy Products Manufacturing & Distribution**: From basic pasteurized milk to premium organic products including UHT milk, yogurt varieties, artisan cheese, traditional ghee, ice cream, and functional dairy beverages
2. **Livestock Trading & Genetics Services**: High-quality cattle trading, artificial insemination services with premium semen, embryo transfer capabilities, and breeding stock development
3. **Equipment & Technology Solutions**: Farm equipment distribution, milking machines, processing machinery, IoT sensors, and smart farming software solutions
4. **Services & Consultancy**: Smart Dairy Academy training programs, mobile veterinary services, fodder solutions, farm consultancy, and financial facilitation services

### 2.2 Bangladesh Dairy Market Analysis

**Macro Market Dynamics:**

Bangladesh's dairy market represents one of the most significant growth opportunities in South Asian agriculture. Despite being one of the world's largest milk-producing nations in absolute terms (ranking among top 20 globally), Bangladesh faces a substantial supply deficit that creates both challenges and opportunities.

**Key Market Statistics:**

| Metric | Current Value | Growth Trend | Source |
|--------|--------------|--------------|--------|
| Total Annual Milk Demand | 14.02 million MT | +7% annually | FAO / BBS |
| Domestic Milk Production | 6.08 million MT | +4% annually | DLS Statistics |
| Supply Deficit | 7.94 million MT (56.5%) | Widening | Industry Analysis |
| Per Capita Consumption | 108 ml/day | Growing | WHO/Bangladesh Health Statistics |
| Global Average | 300+ ml/day | Stable | FAO |
| Number of Dairy Farms | 79,900+ registered | Increasing | Department of Livestock Services |
| Average Herd Size | 3-5 cattle | Gradually increasing | DLS Survey |

**Supply-Demand Gap Analysis:**

The 56.5% supply deficit is currently met through:
- **Powdered Milk Imports**: Approximately 100,000+ tons annually, costing valuable foreign exchange
- **Smuggled Products**: Unregulated dairy products from neighboring countries
- **Informal Sector**: Unprocessed, often adulterated milk from rural sources

This deficit represents an annual market opportunity of approximately BDT 35,000+ Crore that domestic producers can potentially capture through quality improvements, productivity enhancements, and organized supply chain development.

A single, integrated ERP system serving as the definitive source of truth for all business data across all verticals:

**Pillar 5: Ecosystem Connectivity Platform**

Digital infrastructure connecting Smart Dairy with its extended stakeholder network:
- Farmer portal providing service access, information resources, and market prices

**Step 1: Change Initiation**
- Any stakeholder identifies need for change to approved requirements
- Completes Change Request Form documenting:
  - Requestor and date
  - Description of proposed change
  - Business justification
  - Expected impact (scope, time, cost, quality)

- Project team evaluates technical feasibility
- Business analyst assesses process impact
- Project manager estimates schedule and cost implications
- Risk assessment completed

- Steering committee reviews impact assessment

- Approved changes prioritized against project constraints

- Approved changes scheduled in project plan
