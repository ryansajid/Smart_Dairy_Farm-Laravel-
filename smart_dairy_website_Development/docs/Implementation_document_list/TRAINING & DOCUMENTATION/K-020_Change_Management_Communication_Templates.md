## 2. Communication Principles

### 2.1 Transparency

**Definition:** Open, honest, and clear communication about the project

**Practices:**
- Share project timeline, scope, and objectives openly
- Communicate both successes and challenges
- Provide regular updates on progress and setbacks
- Explain the "why" behind decisions

**Implementation:**
- Weekly status updates to all stakeholders
- Open-door policy for questions and concerns
- Accessible project documentation
- Town hall meetings for major announcements

### 2.2 Timeliness

**Definition:** Communication at the right time to maximize impact

**Practices:**
- Advance notice for all significant changes
- Immediate notification for critical issues
- Regular cadence for routine updates
- Time zone consideration for all communications

**Communication Timeline:**

| Event | Advance Notice | Communication Channel |
|-------|---------------|----------------------|
| Major system change | 2 weeks | Email + Meeting |
| Training session | 1 week | Email + Calendar |
| System maintenance | 48 hours | Email + WhatsApp |
| Minor updates | 24 hours | WhatsApp + Intranet |
| Critical issues | Immediate | All channels |

### 2.3 Two-Way Communication

**Definition:** Active listening and response to stakeholder feedback

**Practices:**
- Multiple feedback channels
- Regular pulse surveys
- Q&A sessions
- Anonymous feedback options

### 2.4 Cultural Sensitivity

**Definition:** Respect for Bangladeshi culture, language, and communication preferences

**Practices:**
- Bengali language support for all critical communications
- Respect for local customs and holidays
- Gender-appropriate communication channels
- Hierarchical communication respect

**Cultural Considerations:**

| Aspect | Consideration | Implementation |
|--------|--------------|----------------|
| Language | Bengali is primary | All templates translated |
| Hierarchy | Respect for seniority | Executive communication first |
| Religion | Prayer times | Schedule around prayers |
| Holidays | National holidays | No communication on holidays |
| Gender | Mixed workforce | Gender-inclusive language |

### 8.2 Response Strategies

#### Strategy 1: Education and Communication

**When to use:** Resistance due to lack of information or misinformation

**Approach:**
- Provide clear, consistent information
- Address rumors proactively
- Use multiple communication channels
- Create opportunities for Q&A

**Tactics:**
- Town hall meetings
- FAQ updates
- One-on-one briefings
- Demonstration sessions

#### Strategy 2: Participation and Involvement

**When to use:** Resistance due to feeling excluded or powerless

**Approach:**
- Involve resisters in planning
- Create advisory groups
- Solicit input on solutions
- Assign meaningful roles

**Tactics:**
- User advisory board
- Focus group participation
- Pilot group membership
- Champion roles

#### Strategy 3: Facilitation and Support

**When to use:** Resistance due to adjustment difficulties

**Approach:**
- Provide additional training
- Offer hands-on support
- Adjust workloads temporarily
- Provide stress management resources

**Tactics:**
- One-on-one coaching
- Peer mentoring
- Extended go-live support
- Counseling resources

#### Strategy 4: Negotiation and Agreement

**When to use:** Resistance from powerful stakeholders with legitimate concerns

**Approach:**
- Identify specific concerns
- Explore win-win solutions
- Make reasonable concessions
- Document agreements

**Tactics:**
- Executive negotiation
- Feature prioritization discussions
- Timeline adjustments
- Resource allocation changes

#### Strategy 5: Co-optation

**When to use:** Resistance from influential individuals

**Approach:**
- Give resisters a key role
- Acknowledge their expertise
- Address their concerns
- Convert them to advocates

**Tactics:**
- Advisory roles
- Champion positions
- Recognition opportunities
- Influence on decisions
