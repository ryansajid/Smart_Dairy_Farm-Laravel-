## 2. Brand Story & Positioning

### 2.1 Company Overview

**Smart Dairy Ltd.** is a subsidiary of Smart Group (est. 1998), operating Bangladesh's premier organic dairy farming operation in Narayanganj.

**Key Facts:**
- **Founded:** Part of Smart Group since 1998
- **Current Scale:** 255 cattle, 75 lactating cows, 900 liters/day
- **Growth Vision:** 800 cattle, 3,000 liters/day within 2 years
- **Product Brand:** "Saffron" - 100% organic dairy products
- **Headquarters:** Dhaka, Bangladesh
- **Farm Location:** Narayanganj District

### 2.2 Brand Promise

**"Pure from Farm to Family"**

We deliver 100% organic, antibiotic-free dairy products with complete farm-to-table traceability. Every drop of milk comes from grass-fed cattle raised with ethical, sustainable practices.

### 2.3 Brand Positioning

**Category:** Premium organic dairy products
**Target Market:** Health-conscious urban families in Bangladesh
**Price Position:** Premium (15-25% above conventional dairy)

**Positioning Statement:**
*"For health-conscious Bangladeshi families who demand transparency and quality, Smart Dairy is the organic dairy brand that provides 100% traceable, antibiotic-free products directly from our sustainable farm‚Äîunlike conventional brands that compromise on purity and transparency."*

### 2.4 Brand Values

1. **PURITY** - 100% organic, no antibiotics, no artificial additives
2. **TRANSPARENCY** - Complete traceability from farm to family
3. **SUSTAINABILITY** - Ethical farming, biogas energy, environmental stewardship
4. **QUALITY** - Premium standards in every process
5. **TRUST** - Honest communication, reliable delivery, authentic relationships

### 2.5 Brand Personality

Our brand personality can be described as:

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Natural** | Authentic, genuine, organic, close to nature |
| **Professional** | Reliable, competent, quality-focused |
| **Warm** | Caring, family-oriented, community-minded |
| **Modern** | Tech-enabled, innovative, progressive |
| **Transparent** | Honest, open, traceable, accountable |

**We are:** Natural, reliable, caring, progressive, honest
**We are not:** Artificial, mass-market, impersonal, secretive

## 4. Logo Specifications

### 4.1 Primary Logo

**Description:**
The primary logo consists of a stylized dairy cow icon alongside the wordmark "Smart Dairy" in Playfair Display font, with "100% Organic" seal beneath.

**Components:**
1. Icon: Stylized cow silhouette in primary green
2. Wordmark: "Smart Dairy" in Playfair Display Bold
3. Tagline: "Pure from Farm to Family" in Open Sans
4. Organic seal: "100% Organic" badge

**Color Specification:**
- Primary: Green (#2E7D32) + Neutral-900 (#212121)
- Single color: All green (#2E7D32) or all black (#212121)
- Reversed: White on colored backgrounds

### 4.2 Logo Variants

#### Horizontal Logo (Primary)
```
[Cow Icon] SMART DAIRY
           Pure from Farm to Family
           [100% Organic Seal]
```
- **Minimum Width:** 150px (digital), 40mm (print)
- **Aspect Ratio:** 3:1 approximately
- **Usage:** Website headers, documents, presentations

#### Stacked Logo
```
    [Cow Icon]
   SMART DAIRY
Pure from Farm to Family
  [100% Organic Seal]
```
- **Minimum Width:** 80px (digital), 20mm (print)
- **Aspect Ratio:** 1:1.5 approximately
- **Usage:** Social media profiles, mobile apps, tight spaces

#### Icon Only
```
[Cow Icon with "SD" initials inside]
```
- **Minimum Size:** 32px √ó 32px (digital), 10mm √ó 10mm (print)
- **Usage:** Favicons, app icons, small UI elements

#### Wordmark Only
```
SMART DAIRY
```
- **Usage:** Text-heavy materials when icon is redundant

### 4.3 Clear Space

Maintain clear space around the logo equal to the height of the letter "S" in "SMART" on all sides.

```
        [S-height]
[S-height] [LOGO] [S-height]
        [S-height]
```

**Do not:**
- Place other elements within clear space
- Crop or obscure any part of the logo
- Change spacing between elements

### 4.4 Logo Colors

#### Full Color
- Icon: Primary Green (#2E7D32)
- Wordmark: Neutral-900 (#212121)
- Seal: Gold (#FFD700) with green border

#### Single Color (Green)
- All elements in Primary Green (#2E7D32)
- Use on white or light backgrounds

#### Single Color (Black)
- All elements in Neutral-900 (#212121)
- Use when color printing unavailable

#### Reversed (White)
- All elements in white (#FFFFFF)
- Use on dark or colored backgrounds
- Ensure background contrast ratio > 4.5:1

### 4.5 Logo Don'ts

**Never:**
- ‚ùå Change logo colors outside approved palette
- ‚ùå Rotate or skew the logo
- ‚ùå Add drop shadows, gradients, or effects
- ‚ùå Rearrange or separate logo elements
- ‚ùå Use low-resolution or pixelated versions
- ‚ùå Place logo on busy or low-contrast backgrounds
- ‚ùå Stretch or compress (maintain aspect ratio)
- ‚ùå Outline the logo
- ‚ùå Animate individual logo elements separately

### 4.6 Logo File Formats

**Digital Use:**
- **Primary:** SVG (scalable, best quality)
- **Backup:** PNG with transparent background (300dpi)
- **Web:** WebP for modern browsers

**Print Use:**
- **Vector:** AI, EPS (Adobe Illustrator)
- **Raster:** PNG, TIFF (300dpi minimum)

**Naming Convention:**
```
smart-dairy-logo-[variant]-[color]-[format].[ext]

Examples:
smart-dairy-logo-horizontal-fullcolor.svg
smart-dairy-logo-stacked-white.png
smart-dairy-logo-icon-green.svg
```

## 6. Typography & Hierarchy

### 6.1 Font Families

#### Primary Typeface (English)

**Headings: Playfair Display**
- **Weights:** Regular (400), SemiBold (600), Bold (700)
- **Character:** Elegant, classic, trustworthy, sophisticated
- **Usage:** All headlines, hero text, featured content
- **Source:** Google Fonts

**Body: Open Sans**
- **Weights:** Light (300), Regular (400), SemiBold (600), Bold (700)
- **Character:** Clean, readable, modern, friendly
- **Usage:** Body copy, UI text, buttons, forms
- **Source:** Google Fonts

#### Bengali Typeface

**Headings: Noto Serif Bengali**
- **Weights:** Regular (400), SemiBold (600), Bold (700)
- **Usage:** Bengali headlines

**Body: Noto Sans Bengali**
- **Weights:** Regular (400), SemiBold (600), Bold (700)
- **Usage:** Bengali body text
- **Note:** Use 18px minimum (vs 16px English), line-height 1.8

### 6.2 Type Hierarchy

**English:**
```
H1: Playfair Display Bold, 48px, line-height 1.2
H2: Playfair Display Bold, 38px, line-height 1.3
H3: Playfair Display SemiBold, 30px, line-height 1.3
H4: Playfair Display SemiBold, 24px, line-height 1.4
Body Large: Open Sans Regular, 18px, line-height 1.6
Body: Open Sans Regular, 16px, line-height 1.6
Body Small: Open Sans Regular, 14px, line-height 1.5
```

**Bengali (add 2px to all sizes):**
```
H1: 50px (vs 48px)
H2: 40px (vs 38px)
Body: 18px (vs 16px)
Line height: 1.8 (vs 1.6)
```

## 7. Tone of Voice

### 7.1 Voice Characteristics

Our brand voice is:

| We Are | We Are Not |
|--------|-----------|
| Authentic & Honest | Deceptive or exaggerated |
| Warm & Caring | Cold or corporate |
| Knowledgeable | Condescending or preachy |
| Professional | Overly casual or sloppy |
| Clear & Direct | Jargon-heavy or confusing |

### 7.2 Tone Guidelines

**English Communications:**
- Use active voice: "We deliver fresh milk daily" (not "Fresh milk is delivered")
- Be conversational but professional
- Use "you" and "we" to create connection
- Keep sentences concise (aim for 15-20 words)
- Avoid dairy industry jargon unless explaining it

**Bengali Communications:**
- Use formal register (‡¶Ü‡¶™‡¶®‡¶ø not ‡¶§‡ßÅ‡¶Æ‡¶ø)
- Maintain respectful, professional tone
- Use simple, clear Bangla (avoid overly Sanskritized words)
- Provide context for English loanwords if necessary

#### Good Examples:

**Homepage Hero:**
"Fresh organic milk, delivered to your door every morning. 100% pure, 100% traceable."

**Product Description:**
"Our organic whole milk comes from grass-fed cattle on our Narayanganj farm. No antibiotics. No hormones. Just pure, creamy goodness‚Äîthe way milk should be."

**Error Message:**
"We couldn't process your payment. Please check your details and try again, or contact our support team."

#### Bad Examples:

**Too Corporate:**
‚ùå "Smart Dairy Ltd. leverages cutting-edge agricultural methodologies to optimize bovine lactation productivity."

**Better:**
‚úÖ "We use modern farming techniques to help our cows produce the best milk possible."

**Too Casual:**

### 7.4 Messaging Pillars

**1. Purity**
- "100% organic, every drop"
- "No antibiotics, no hormones, no compromises"
- "Pure milk, pure ingredients, pure trust"

**2. Transparency**
- "Know your farm, know your food"
- "From our pastures to your table‚Äîwith nothing to hide"
- "Complete traceability in every bottle"

**3. Quality**
- "Premium dairy, ethically raised"
- "The finest organic dairy in Bangladesh"
- "Quality you can taste, standards you can trust"

**4. Sustainability**
- "Good for you, good for the planet"
- "Ethical farming, sustainable future"
- "Caring for our cattle, our community, our earth"

### 7.5 Word Choices

**Use:**
- Organic, natural, pure, fresh
- Farm, pasture, grass-fed, free-range
- Sustainable, ethical, responsible
- Family, community, neighbors
- Traceable, transparent, honest

**Avoid:**
- Artificial, synthetic, processed
- Factory, industrial, mass-produced
- Cheap, budget, economy
- Corporate jargon (leverage, synergy, etc.)
- Exaggerations (best in the world, perfect, etc.)

## 8. Photography Style

### 8.1 Visual Style

Our photography style is **Documentary + Lifestyle + Premium**.

**Characteristics:**
- Natural, authentic, unposed moments
- Natural lighting (golden hour preferred)
- Warm color tones (slightly desaturated, earthy)
- Shallow depth of field for product shots
- Wide environmental shots for farm scenes

### 8.2 Subject Matter

**Primary Subjects:**
1. **Cattle & Farm Animals**
   - Healthy, clean cattle in pastures
   - Close-ups showing care and welfare
   - Calves with mothers (emotional connection)
   - Show spacious, clean environments

2. **Farm & Facilities**
   - Wide shots of green pastures
   - Clean, modern milking facilities
   - Biogas plant and sustainability features
   - Farm workers caring for animals

3. **Products**
   - Milk bottles with condensation (freshness)
   - Yogurt cups with fresh fruit
   - Butter and ghee with natural lighting
   - Cheese with artisanal presentation

4. **People**
   - Happy families (urban, middle-class)
   - Children drinking milk / eating yogurt
   - Farm workers (dignified, professional)
   - Chefs and food preparation

5. **Lifestyle**
   - Breakfast tables with dairy products
   - Mothers preparing meals
   - Delivery to homes (convenience)
   - Farmers markets / community

### 8.3 Photography Do's

**Do:**
- ‚úÖ Use natural light whenever possible
- ‚úÖ Shoot during golden hour (sunrise/sunset)
- ‚úÖ Show real people, real moments
- ‚úÖ Capture genuine emotions
- ‚úÖ Show the farm environment authentically
- ‚úÖ Use shallow depth of field for products
- ‚úÖ Include green/natural backgrounds
- ‚úÖ Show cleanliness and care

### 8.4 Photography Don'ts

**Don't:**
- ‚ùå Use stock photos of generic farms/cattle
- ‚ùå Over-edit or heavily filter images
- ‚ùå Show industrial/factory-like environments
- ‚ùå Use harsh artificial lighting
- ‚ùå Stage overly perfect, unnatural scenes
- ‚ùå Show unhealthy or stressed animals
- ‚ùå Use clich√©d dairy imagery (milk splash, etc.)

### 8.5 Image Treatment

**Color Grading:**
```
Highlights: +5 to +10 (slightly lifted)
Shadows: +10 to +15 (avoid crushing blacks)
Saturation: -10 to -15 (slightly desaturated)
Warmth: +5 to +10 (warm, inviting tones)
Greens: +10 (enhance pasture greens)
```

**Preferred Filters/Presets:**
- Natural, warm tones
- Slight fade in shadows
- Enhanced greens
- Soft contrast (not harsh)

**Tools:**
- Adobe Lightroom (preferred)
- VSCO (mobile)
- Capture One (professional)

### 8.6 Image Specifications

**Web Use:**
- Format: WebP (primary), JPG (fallback)
- Resolution: 1920px width maximum
- Compression: 80-85% quality
- File size: < 200KB per image

**Social Media:**
- Instagram: 1080x1080px (square), 1080x1350px (portrait)
- Facebook: 1200x630px (link preview)
- YouTube Thumbnail: 1280x720px

**Print:**
- Resolution: 300dpi minimum
- Format: TIFF or high-quality JPG
- Color space: CMYK (for print)

## 9. Imagery Guidelines

### 9.1 Illustration Style

When photographs aren't suitable, use illustrations that are:

**Style Characteristics:**
- Line art with selective color fills
- Primary green (#2E7D32) + neutral tones
- Simple, clean, modern aesthetic
- Friendly, approachable character designs

**Usage:**
- Empty states in apps/website
- Explanatory diagrams (farm-to-table process)
- Iconography and spot illustrations
- Educational content

### 9.2 Icons

See [A-015: Iconography & Illustration Guidelines](A-015_Iconography-Illustration-Guidelines.md) for complete specifications.

**Icon Style:**
- Primary: Font Awesome 6.4
- Custom icons: Line style, 2px stroke, rounded caps
- Colors: Green (#2E7D32) or Neutral-700 (#616161)

### 9.3 Patterns & Textures

**Approved Patterns:**
- Subtle grass texture (10% opacity)
- Light geometric grid (representing farm rows)
- Organic shapes (leaf patterns, very subtle)

**Usage:**
- Background textures for sections
- Decorative headers
- Page dividers

**Don't:**
- ‚ùå Use busy patterns that compete with content
- ‚ùå Use patterns in primary content areas
- ‚ùå Reduce readability with background textures

### 9.4 Video Guidelines

**Style:**
- Documentary style with natural audio
- Drone shots of farm (wide establishing shots)
- Handheld for intimate moments
- Stabilized for product shots

**Specs:**
- Resolution: 1080p minimum (4K for hero videos)
- Framerate: 30fps (24fps for cinematic feel)
- Aspect ratio: 16:9 (web), 1:1 or 9:16 (social)
- Length: 15-30 seconds (social), 60-90 seconds (website)

### 12.2 Naming Conventions

`smart-dairy-logo-[variant]-[color]-[size].[ext]`

**Photos:**
`smartdairy-[category]-[description]-[date].[ext]`

`smartdairy-template-[platform]-[type].[ext]`

**Internal Teams:**

### 13.2 Brand Approval Process

**Required Approval For:**
- New marketing campaigns
- Product packaging
- Website redesigns
- Major social media initiatives
- Partnership collateral

**Approval Workflow:**
1. Submit to design team for review
2. Design team provides feedback
3. Revisions completed
4. Marketing director approves
5. Launch/publish
